Our Story

We, at Transform Her, sat down did some research and came up with some findings: Gender inequality is a glaring issue in Africa. Despite many solutions carried out to bridge the gap, the problem still persists. In most communities, women are not given equal opportunity as men for growth and development. This has worsen with the Covid-19 pandemic. It is worth noting that, empowerment of women and girls is essential to expand economic growth and promote social development. We came up with adept solutions to help solve these issues on gender inequality; empowering women/girls to attain career success is pivotal mainly focusing on STEM courses & jobs.
We intend to solve this STEM gap by carrying out aggressive sensitization of girls (12 to 35 years) and their parents/guardians in both rural and urban communities: including the poorest communities. Train as many girls as possible in STEM related fields, and get them mentored by high profile women. One of the major ways girls can be encouraged to build career in STEM is hearing high profile females tell their success stories. These girls will uphold them as role models, feel encouraged and motivated to be like them. That is why we interview these high profile women and make the videos available to our target market.
